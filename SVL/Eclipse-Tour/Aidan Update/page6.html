Check out the Path of Totality

As the moon orbits around the Earth through the eclipse, its shadow leaves a crescent or banana shaped path on the surface of the Earth. However, the sides of this path are lighter than the center. Within this region known as the penumbra shown within the pink lines, the day will still turn dark, but the sun will not be fully covered. The darker center of the path, shown in blue, is caused by the moon’s umbra shadow. It’s in the umbra that you can see the total solar eclipse and these places will turn dark during totality.

Use the navigation buttons on the right to zoom in and see if your house is in the path of totality! Use the video control buttons to watch the eclipse move across the globe.
